    InteractWBL - a MSc Software Development project

    The purpose of this application is to facilitate the delivery of a work-based learning Graduate Apprenticeship.

    WBL proposes that learning should take place in combination with, and through work – its sole aim being to maximize learning. Learning would be maximized because applying knowledge and skills in the context of the workplace – with immediate practical outcomes– the learning experience is more meaningful and engaging, and so the individual is more likely to appreciate and thus retain the knowledge gained in the process.

    With work-based learning, a great deal of importance is put on the personal experience of the learner – and not adventitiously so, because, as McLennan & Keating remark, work-based learning has its origins in experiential learning theory (ELT).

    Pioneered by American John Dewey, experiential learning theory attempts to clarify what types of experiences are most meaningful and engaging.
